NAIC Principles for Effective Cybersecurity

The National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) adopted principles for effective cybersecurity insurance regulatory guidance. High Tide Technology has extensive experience helping insurance companies address these principles and ensure compliance.

Regulatory Responsibility

State insurance regulators have a responsibility to ensure that personally identifiable consumer information held by insurers, producers, and other regulated entities is protected from cybersecurity risks.

Data Safeguarding

Confidential and/or personally identifiable consumer information collected, stored, and transferred inside or outside a regulated entity's network should be appropriately safeguarded.

Investigative Authority

State insurance regulators have the authority to investigate cybersecurity events at regulated entities, with technical capabilities to do so and coordination with other agencies as appropriate.

Flexible, Risk-Based Guidance

Cybersecurity regulatory guidance should be flexible, scalable, practical, and consistent with nationally recognized efforts such as the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.

Resource-Proportionate Requirements

Regulatory guidance must be risk-based and consider the resources of the regulated entity, with the caveat that cybersecurity risks must be specifically identified.

Regulatory Oversight

State insurance regulators should provide appropriate oversight including risk-based financial examinations and market conduct examinations regarding cybersecurity.

Third-Party Data Responsibility

Insurers, producers, and other regulated entities are responsible for the security of personal data they hold, even if held by a third-party service provider.

Third-Party Vendor Security

Regulated entities should take appropriate steps to ensure that third-party service providers have adequate cybersecurity measures in place to protect personally identifiable information.

Enterprise Risk Management Integration

Cybersecurity risks should be incorporated and addressed as part of an insurer's Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) process.

Board-Level Reporting

Information technology internal audit findings that present a material risk to an insurer should be reviewed with the board of directors or appropriate committee thereof.

Employee Training

It is the responsibility of regulated entities to ensure that their employees and agents are trained to identify cybersecurity issues and to minimize security gaps.

Law Enforcement Collaboration

Regulated entities should join forces with law enforcement to combat cyber attacks, maintaining an incident response plan that includes processes for notifying law enforcement.
